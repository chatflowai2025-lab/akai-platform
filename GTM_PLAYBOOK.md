## 1. ICP Definition — Who Is the Perfect AKAI Customer?

### Primary ICP — "The Overwhelmed Owner" (Fastest to close, highest LTV)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Industry** | High-ticket service businesses: luxury home services (kitchens, bathrooms, landscaping), marine/yachts, property/real estate, financial advisors, recruitment agencies |
| **Size** | 3–25 employees, $500K–$5M annual revenue |
| **Geography** | Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane (Phase 1) — then AU-wide, then US |
| **Pain points** | Spending 10–20hrs/week on admin, follow-ups, quoting, scheduling; no dedicated marketing person; spending $2K–$5K/mo on piecemeal tools (MailChimp, Calendly, LinkedIn ads, VA) that don't talk to each other |
| **Budget** | Can spend $500–$1,500/mo without committee approval — owner signs off solo |
| **Tech-savviness** | Uses iPhone, Xero or MYOB, maybe HubSpot or no CRM at all. Not a tech person — but not afraid of it if results are obvious |
| **Decision trigger** | Just lost a big lead they forgot to follow up, or just fired a VA, or just had a terrible month for new business |
| **Ideal first plan** | Growth ($497/mo) — gives them enough horsepower to feel the difference |

**Why they close fast:** The pain is personal (it's their money, their stress), the decision is theirs alone, and the ROI is immediately tangible — one recovered lead pays for 3 months.

| **Enterprise / corporates (200+ employees)** | Too slow to decide, want custom integrations, require legal/procurement sign-off, want SLAs you can't support yet |
| **Pure e-commerce / product businesses** | AKAI is built for service businesses with sales conversations — not Shopify stores |
| **Tech-resistant owners (60+, paper-based)** | Will need too much hand-holding. High support cost, low retention |

## 7. The Monday Meeting — ON Group (30 March)

### Background
Ron Stolikas runs ON Group. This is an active prospect, not a cold lead — the meeting is already booked, which means there's genuine interest. The question is how to close, not how to get in the room.

### What ON Group Likely Is
A professional services or operations business that's reached the point of needing systems — not just people. Ron is probably evaluating AKAI against: doing nothing, hiring someone, or buying a cheaper point solution.

### The Ask
**Be explicit. Don't leave Monday without one of these outcomes:**
1. Signed on Growth or Scale plan — credit card or invoice issued
2. Clear next step with a hard date (e.g., "I'll send you the agreement by Wednesday and you'll sign by Friday")
3. A no — with the specific reason documented so you can address or move on

**Ideal outcome:** Growth plan ($497/mo) or Scale ($1,497/mo) signed on the day, or within 48 hours. Do not accept "I'll think about it" without a follow-up date locked in.

### The Meeting Structure (30–45 minutes)

**Minutes 1–5: Discovery first**
Don't demo immediately. Ask:
- "Before I show you anything, what's the one thing costing ON Group the most time or money right now?"
- "What does your current sales/follow-up/admin process look like on a weekly basis?"
- Let Ron talk. Listen for the pain.

**Minutes 5–20: Targeted demo**
Show only what's relevant to his pain. If he said "follow-up is a mess" → show Sales Agent + Email Agent. If he said "we're drowning in proposals" → show Proposals Agent. Don't show all 10 agents. Show 2–3 that solve *his* problem.

**Minutes 20–30: ROI moment**
> "Ron, based on what you've told me — if we recovered just [X leads/jobs/clients] per month that are currently slipping through, what's that worth to ON Group?"
>
> Let him name the number. Then: "AKAI costs $497 a month. What you just described is worth [$X]. That's a [Y]x return."

**Minutes 30–45: Close**
> "I'd love to get you started. We can have you fully set up by the end of this week. What's the best way to proceed — invoice or card?"

If he hesitates, ask: "What's the one thing that would need to be true for you to move forward today?"

### Objections Ron Will Raise + How to Handle Them

**Objection 1: "We're not sure we're ready for AI / we're not very tech-savvy"**
> "That's exactly why we built it this way. You don't touch the AI. It runs in the background. What you experience is: leads get followed up, meetings get booked, proposals go out. I set it up with you in the first week. You just run your business."

**Objection 2: "We already have [HubSpot / Zoho / some CRM]"**
> "AKAI doesn't replace your CRM — it's the thing that actually fills it. Most CRMs are great at storing data. They're terrible at acting on it. AKAI is the action layer. We can pipe leads and updates straight into [their tool] if needed."

**Objection 3: "The price is a lot — can we start smaller?"**
> "Growth is $497 a month. If you have even one job a month that didn't close because of slow follow-up, and that job is worth $10K, AKAI paid for itself 20x. The risk isn't the $497 — it's another month of losing leads.
>
> That said — Starter at $147 gets you 3 core agents. If you want to start there and upgrade in 60 days, that works too. But most businesses in your position who start on Starter are on Growth within 6 weeks anyway."

**Objection 4: "We need to think about it / talk to my partner"**
> "Totally understand. What's the one thing you'd need clarity on before moving forward? Let me address that now so you don't have to wait.
>
> And if you want to rope your partner in — is there a 20-minute window this week where all three of us could connect? I can have a one-pager ready for them in advance."

**Objection 5: "How do we know it actually works? It all sounds great in theory"**
> "Fair question. AP Heritage Interior — luxury kitchens, Sydney — recovered $180K in pipeline in their first 60 days. Sydney Harbour Exclusive — yacht brokers — said it was like hiring a full-time sales coordinator for $500 a month. These aren't case study simulations — these are businesses I can introduce you to if you want a real conversation.
>
> I'll also set you up in the first week and you'll see it working in your business before the end of Month 1. If it doesn't deliver, we'll talk."

**Objection 6: "What happens if we want to cancel?"**
> "Month-to-month. No lock-in, no cancellation fees. I'd rather earn your business every month than trap you into a contract. The ones who stay are the ones who see results — and that's what I'm focused on."
