**Confidential | Version 1.0 | March 2026**
**Prepared by:** AKAI Executive Team (CMO + Research)

## Executive Summary

AKAI is an AI Business Operating System built for SMBs who are done being overcharged by agencies and underwhelmed by DIY tools. Five modules — **Sales, Recruit, Web, Ads, Social** — deliver what a full-service digital agency does, at a fraction of the cost, with zero hand-holding required.

The window is now. AI infrastructure has matured to the point where a $297/month product can outperform a $5,000/month agency engagement. The SMBs who figure this out first will dominate their local markets. AKAI puts them there.

**12-month target:** 200 customers, $30K MRR.

## 1. Market Opportunity

### 1.1 Total Addressable Market

#### Australia
- **2.59 million** active small businesses as of June 2025 (ABS)
- 97.2% of all Australian businesses are classified as small
- Projected to reach ~3M by 2030 at 1.5-2% annual growth
- SMEs typically spend **$1,500–$5,000 AUD/month** on digital marketing services alone (eWebMarketing, 2025)
- **80% of Australian SMBs** now use generative AI in at least one business function (Microsoft/IDC, Feb 2026)
- AI adoption grew 43% YoY — the market is primed, not skeptical

**AU Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):** AKAI targets tech-aware SMBs in trades, retail, professional services, hospitality, and health — approximately **400,000 businesses** in priority verticals nationally.

**AU Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1:** 200 customers = 0.05% of SAM. Very conservative. Very achievable.

#### United States
- **33 million** small businesses (SBA, 2025)
- Average SMB spends **$10,000–$50,000/year** on marketing services
- US SMB digital advertising spend projected at **$42B in 2025** (eMarketer)
- AI automation adoption accelerating — 78% of SMBs globally use AI in at least one function

**US SAM:** Same verticals as AU — approximately **5 million businesses**. Phase 2/3 priority.

### 1.2 Key Pain Points (Research-Validated)

1. **Agency pricing opacity** — One AU agency quotes $1,500/month, another $15,000. For the same work. SMBs have no way to know if they're getting value.
2. **Lock-in without results** — 6-12 month contracts with agencies, often producing minimal measurable lead flow.
3. **Time-to-launch** — Average agency website build: 6-12 weeks. AKAI Web: same day.
4. **Fragmentation** — SMBs use 5-8 separate tools for CRM, ads, social, email, recruitment. Switching costs, training overhead, and data silos kill productivity.
5. **DIY tools don't convert** — Wix/Squarespace websites look generic. GoDaddy leads to Google. Nothing closes.
6. **No follow-up** — 80% of leads are lost due to slow or absent follow-up. Most SMBs have no system.
7. **Recruitment costs exploding** — Average cost to hire in Australia: $23,000+ (AHRI, 2024). Agencies charge 15-20% of first-year salary. Unworkable for SMBs.

### 1.3 Why Now

Three forces are converging that make 2026 the exact right moment:

1. **AI infrastructure is production-ready.** GPT-4o, voice AI, and automation tools have crossed the reliability threshold. What required a $500K enterprise platform in 2022 now costs $200/month in API fees. AKAI's cost to deliver is negligible vs. perceived value.

2. **Agency rates never dropped.** Despite AI reducing the cost of producing content, websites, and ads by 80%+, agencies haven't passed savings to clients. The gap between what agencies charge and what it costs them to deliver is AKAI's margin.

3. **SMB owners are AI-aware but AI-overwhelmed.** 80% are experimenting with AI tools, but they're drowning in options. They want a system, not more subscriptions. AKAI is the system.

4. **Post-COVID operational lean mentality.** Australian SMBs emerged from 2020-2023 with a permanent sensitivity to cost efficiency. They're not going back to $5K/month retainers.

## 3. Module Pricing Strategy

### 3.1 Market Benchmarks

| Category | DIY Tools (Low) | AKAI Target | Traditional Agency (High) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Website | $12–$29/mo (Wix, Durable, Squarespace) | $97–$197/mo | $3,000–$10,000 build + $500/mo |
| Sales/CRM Automation | $19–$99/mo (HubSpot, Pipedrive) | $197–$397/mo | $2,500–$5,000/mo retainer |
| Recruitment ATS | $49–$169/user/mo (Recruiterflow, Kula) | $297–$597/mo | 15-20% of first-year salary per hire |
| Social Media Mgmt | $15–$99/mo (Buffer, Hootsuite) | $97–$197/mo | $1,500–$3,000/mo |
| Paid Ads Mgmt | $50–$200/mo (DIY tools) | $197–$397/mo | $1,500–$3,500/mo + 15% ad spend |

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **Durable:** $12–$22/month — pure DIY website builder. No lead gen, no sales follow-up.
- **Lindy.ai:** $29.99–$49.99/month — general AI automation. Not SMB-specific, steep learning curve.
- **11x.ai:** $5,000–$20,000+/month (enterprise AI SDR). Priced out of SMB reach entirely.
- **Webflow:** $16–$49/month — developer-oriented. Requires technical skills.
- **Bubble:** $29–$349/month — app builder. Complex. Not SMB-appropriate.

### 3.2 AKAI Pricing Architecture

**Positioning:** 40–60% below agency rates. 20–30% above DIY tools. Value = agency outcomes + DIY simplicity.

### 3.3 Pricing Psychology

- **Never lead with price.** Lead with the problem: "You're spending $2,500/month with an agency and getting 3 leads. AKAI gets you 15 leads for $397/month."
- **Anchor to agency cost.** Every sales conversation frames AKAI against what they're currently paying (or should be paying) an agency.
- **Show ROI, not features.** If AKAI brings in 1 extra customer/month worth $1,500 in revenue — it's already paid for itself 3x.

## 8. Competitive Analysis

### Direct Competitors

| Competitor | What They Do | Pricing | Gap AKAI Fills |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Durable** | AI website builder, 30-second site generation | $12–$22/mo | No lead gen, no CRM, no follow-up. A website is not a system. |
| **Webflow** | Design-code website builder, developer-focused | $16–$49/mo | Requires technical skill. SMB owner can't use it. No sales automation. |
| **Bubble** | No-code app builder | $29–$349/mo | Builds apps, not lead pipelines. Complex, steep learning curve. |
| **11x.ai** | AI SDR (outbound sales reps) | $5,000–$20,000+/mo | Enterprise-only. SMBs can't afford it. No local AU focus. |
| **Lindy.ai** | General AI automation agents | $29.99–$49.99/mo | Horizontal tool, not SMB-specific. Requires setup expertise. No voice. No recruitment. |

### AKAI's Unfair Advantages

1. **Vertical depth:** AKAI is purpose-built for SMBs. Every prompt, workflow, and UI decision assumes a non-technical owner.
2. **Voice-first with Sophie:** Human-sounding AI phone agent is a proven channel competitors don't deploy.
3. **Australia-first:** Local numbers, local examples, AEST support. SMBs trust local.
4. **Full-stack:** No competitor offers Sales + Recruit + Web + Ads + Social under one roof at SMB pricing.
5. **Proven core:** AI Clozr is already working. This isn't pre-product speculation.

### Key Risks
- **Feature copycats:** Durable, HubSpot, and others are adding AI features rapidly. Moat is brand, relationships, and vertical specialisation — not tech exclusivity.
- **SMB churn:** SMBs go out of business. Need to replace churned customers with quality, not just volume.
- **US market unfamiliarity:** Cold entry without warm network. Need a local partner or strategic hire.

## 9. Launch Checklist

What needs to be done before the first paying AKAI customer.

### Brand & Identity
- [ ] AKAI domain registered and live (akai.ai or akai.com.au — check availability)
- [ ] Logo, brand colours, typography confirmed (not just "good enough" — this is premium positioning)
- [ ] One-sentence elevator pitch finalised: "AKAI is your AI business operating system — Sales, Web, Ads, Recruit, and Social in one platform, for the cost of a part-time junior."
- [ ] Brand applied to: website, email signatures, proposal template, invoice template

### Website
- [ ] Landing page live with clear hero, value prop, pricing, and CTA
- [ ] Module-specific subpages (at minimum: AKAI Sales)
- [ ] Booking link embedded (Calendly or equivalent)
- [ ] Payment processing set up (Stripe preferred)
- [ ] Legal: Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Refund Policy
- [ ] SSL, mobile-optimised, fast loading

### Product (AKAI Sales / AI Clozr)
- [ ] Rebranded to AKAI (UI, emails, dashboard)
- [ ] Onboarding flow documented and tested (can a non-technical owner complete it in <30 mins?)
- [ ] Customer success checklist created (what does Month 1 success look like?)
- [ ] Dashboard shows clear ROI metrics (leads generated, calls made, conversations booked)
- [ ] Billing integrated and tested

### Sales Process
- [ ] Sophie script written and tested for target verticals
- [ ] Outbound call list sourced (min. 1,000 businesses for Month 1)
- [ ] CRM set up to track leads, demos, and closes
- [ ] Demo script / deck prepared (short — 10 min max, results-first)
- [ ] Proposal template ready
- [ ] Contract template reviewed by a lawyer (AU compliant)
- [ ] Referral program mechanics defined and communicated

### Support & Operations
- [ ] Customer support email or channel active (Intercom, Crisp, or equivalent)
- [ ] Customer onboarding email sequence written (Days 1, 3, 7, 14, 30)
- [ ] Response SLA defined (≤24 hours for AU business hours)
- [ ] Basic FAQ page or help docs published
- [ ] Escalation path for product issues

### Analytics
- [ ] Google Analytics or equivalent on all pages
- [ ] MRR tracking dashboard live (even a Google Sheet is fine to start)
- [ ] Weekly reporting cadence defined: new customers, churn, MRR, CAC

### Go/No-Go Criteria for Launch
All of the following must be true:
- [ ] Product works end-to-end without manual intervention
- [ ] At least 1 customer has been through the full onboarding (beta/pilot)
- [ ] Payment processing is tested with a real transaction
- [ ] Sophie can make outbound calls in test mode
- [ ] Aaron can deliver a 10-minute demo from memory
